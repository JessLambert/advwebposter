Net Neutrality is the way the internet has always existed (at least in the US). You can visit any site you’d like, at any time, and only interact with features that you choose to. Most importantly, you can share and find information without corporate interference or privacy invasions.

Companies and corporate interest want to change this - without net neutrality, they would be free to charge for you to access certain sites, and potentially limit the information you could access altogether.

Don't suffer the buffer.

Remember in the old days of dial up when you’d leave the room and your screen would still be loading? We could be headed back to the Stone Age.

What if all those Wikipedia popups asking for money were paywalls? You’d never get your homework done.

Sites still have the option of putting in paywalls to support their companies, but what if you had to pay your provider directly every time you wanted to watch Netflix? Parties hosting their sites could be charged for [Insert Provider Here]’s customers to have access to the site. 3.

Speech

matters.

So maybe you’re pissed off at your provider, and you want to see if anyone else is having the same problems you are. But those results won’t even appear for you.

The beauty of the internet is the extreme freedom. If you want to look at My Little Pony porn, the internet doesn’t judge you. If you want to voice your discontent with a service, the internet welcomes this and can even connect you with others just as annoyed. Most importantly, information can be shared without interference, so that the truth is available to every- one. Being able to hear the voices of others without censorship has turned the internet into a powerful platform for social change.
